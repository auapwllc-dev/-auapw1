Nissan History

Nissan — Complete Brand History

Nissan Motor Co., Ltd. traces its roots to 1914 when Masujiro Hashimoto founded Kwaishinsha Motor Car Works in Tokyo. The DAT Motors company was formed in 1926, whose small car was called the Datson (son of DAT), later renamed Datsun meaning son of DAT. Yoshisuke Aikawa restructured the company in 1933 into Jidosha-Seizo Co., Ltd., which was renamed Nissan Motor Co., Ltd. in 1934. Nissan sold vehicles in America under the Datsun name from 1958 to 1981, when it was officially renamed Nissan in the US. The Z-car series debuted in 1969 with the 240Z — widely regarded as one of the greatest sports cars of all time, credited with defeating the British sports car monopoly in America. The Sentra, Altima, and Maxima became bread-and-butter models for Nissan in America. The Pathfinder and Frontier serve truck buyers. The GT-R (Godzilla) debuted in its current form in 2007 and delivered supercar performance at a fraction of supercar prices, accelerating 0-60 in 2.9 seconds. Nissan partnered with Renault in 1999 forming the Renault-Nissan Alliance, later joined by Mitsubishi. Nissan introduced the Leaf in 2010 as the world's first mass-market all-electric vehicle. The Rogue is Nissan's best-selling model in America, competing in the crowded compact SUV segment. Nissan produced the legendary SR20DET, VQ35DE, and RB26DETT engines beloved by enthusiasts worldwide.

Nissan Parts FAQ

Are Nissan CVT transmissions reliable?
Nissan CVTs have a mixed reliability record. Older Jatco CVT7 units in 2013-2017 Altima and Sentra had higher failure rates. The newer CVT8 in post-2018 models is more reliable. Extended warranty coverage from Nissan was provided for many affected models. AUAPW.ORG sources tested CVT units with 30-90 day warranties.
How reliable is the VQ35 engine?
The VQ35DE and VQ35HR are among Nissan's most reliable engines, commonly reaching 200,000+ miles. The VQ35HR in the 350Z/370Z and G35/G37 is particularly beloved by enthusiasts for its high-revving character and durability. AUAPW.ORG regularly sources these engines.
