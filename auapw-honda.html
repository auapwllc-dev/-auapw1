Honda History

Honda — Complete Brand History

Honda Motor Co., Ltd. was founded on September 24, 1948, by Soichiro Honda in Hamamatsu, Shizuoka, Japan. Honda began as a manufacturer of motorcycles, attaching war-surplus engines to bicycles. The company entered automobile production in 1963 with the T360 mini truck and S500 sports car. Honda entered the American market in 1969. The Civic launched in 1972 and became Honda's foundation in America, earning a reputation for reliability and fuel efficiency. The Accord launched in 1976 and has been one of America's best-selling cars for decades. Honda is notably the world's largest manufacturer of internal combustion engines, producing over 30 million per year across motorcycles, cars, marine engines, generators, and power equipment. Honda introduced the VTEC variable valve timing system in 1989, which dramatically improved engine efficiency and performance. The NSX supercar debuted in 1990 and shocked Ferrari with its everyday usability combined with supercar performance. The CR-V launched in 1995 and became one of America's most popular compact SUVs. The Odyssey minivan is consistently rated the best minivan in America. Honda's Acura luxury division launched in 1986 as the first Japanese luxury brand in America. Honda pioneered the CVCC engine which met 1975 US emission standards without a catalytic converter. The Honda S2000 sports car is considered one of the finest driver's cars ever made.

Frequently Asked Questions

Honda Parts FAQ

Are Honda engines known for reliability?
Honda engines are among the most reliable in the world. The K24 2.4L engine in Accord and CR-V is legendary for 200,000+ mile longevity. The J35 3.5L V6 in Pilot and Odyssey is also exceptionally durable. Honda's attention to manufacturing tolerances results in engines that outlast most competitors.
What Honda transmissions are available used?
The most commonly sourced Honda transmissions are the 5-speed automatic from Civic and Accord (2003-2012), the 6-speed manual from Civic Si, the 6-speed automatic from Accord V6, and the CVT from recent Civic and CR-V models. AUAPW.ORG can source any of these.
What is the most common Honda engine failure?
The most common issues include oil consumption on 2008-2012 Accord 4-cylinders, timing chain wear on 2.4L K24 engines at high mileage, and head gasket issues on 2001-2005 V6 Odyssey and Pilot. AUAPW.ORG inspects for these issues before sourcing replacement units.
