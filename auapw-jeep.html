Jeep History

Jeep — Complete Brand History

Jeep's origins trace back to 1940 when the US Army issued a requirement for a light reconnaissance vehicle. Karl Probst designed the original Jeep prototype for Willys-Overland in just 18 hours. The Willys MB military Jeep was produced from 1941-1945, with over 640,000 units built for WWII. General Eisenhower called it one of the three most decisive weapons of WWII. The CJ (Civilian Jeep) launched in 1945 immediately after the war, becoming the world's first mass-market off-road vehicle. Kaiser-Jeep Corporation acquired Willys in 1963. American Motors Corporation (AMC) acquired Jeep in 1970. Chrysler Corporation acquired AMC and Jeep in 1987. The Grand Cherokee debuted in 1992 and revolutionized the SUV market by offering luxury features with genuine off-road capability. The term SUV (Sport Utility Vehicle) was popularized largely thanks to Jeep. Jeep created the Trail Rated certification program to verify genuine off-road capability across five metrics: traction, ground clearance, maneuverability, articulation, and water fording. The Wrangler maintains a body-on-frame construction and solid front axle — qualities most SUVs have abandoned — making it uniquely capable off-road. The Gladiator pickup truck returned in 2019, combining Wrangler capability with truck utility. The Grand Wagoneer revived a legendary nameplate in 2021 as Jeep's flagship luxury SUV.

Jeep Parts FAQ

What Jeep axles are available used?
The Dana 44 (front and rear on Wrangler Rubicon), Dana 30 (front on Wrangler Sport/Sahara), Dana 35 (rear on older Wranglers), Dana 60 (Wrangler 392), and Chrysler 9.25 and 8.25 (Grand Cherokee) are all commonly available. AUAPW.ORG sources axle assemblies with ring gear, bearings, and seals.
Are Jeep 3.6L Pentastar engines reliable?
The 3.6L Pentastar V6 introduced in 2011 is generally reliable, commonly reaching 150,000-200,000 miles with proper maintenance. Early versions had known issues with valve train noise and catalytic converter failures. Post-2014 versions addressed most concerns. AUAPW.ORG sources inspected Pentastar units.
What is the most common Jeep transfer case?
The NP231 (older Wrangler and Cherokee), NP242 (Grand Cherokee), NV241 Rock-Trac (Rubicon), and NP246 (Grand Cherokee 5.7L) are the most commonly replaced. The NV241 Rock-Trac is the most capable. AUAPW.ORG stocks transfer case assemblies for all Jeep models.
