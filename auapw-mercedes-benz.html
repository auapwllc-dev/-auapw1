Mercedes-Benz History

Mercedes-Benz — Complete Brand History

Mercedes-Benz has the distinction of having its origins in the very invention of the automobile. Karl Benz was granted Patent DRP 37435 on January 29, 1886, for the Benz Patent-Motorwagen — widely considered the world's first true automobile. Simultaneously, Gottlieb Daimler and Wilhelm Maybach developed their own gasoline-powered vehicles. The name "Mercedes" was first used in 1901 when businessman Emil Jellinek commissioned Daimler to build a car named after his daughter Mercedes. Daimler-Motoren-Gesellschaft and Benz & Cie. merged on June 28, 1926, creating Daimler-Benz AG and the Mercedes-Benz brand. The three-pointed star logo symbolizes motorization of land, sea, and air. Mercedes-Benz invented the first diesel passenger car in 1936 (260 D). The legendary 300 SL Gullwing of 1954 introduced fuel injection to production cars. Mercedes pioneered the crumple zone safety technology in 1951, saving millions of lives. The S-Class has continuously been the technological flagship of the automotive industry — features now standard across all cars were introduced on the S-Class decades earlier, including ABS (1978), airbags (1980), ESP electronic stability control (1995), and pre-collision systems. AMG was founded in 1967 as an independent tuning company and acquired by Mercedes in 1999. Today Mercedes-Benz produces the C-Class, E-Class, S-Class, GLC, GLE, GLS, AMG GT, and a growing lineup of EQ electric vehicles.

Are Mercedes-Benz engines reliable when used?
The M272 V6 and M273 V8 engines from 2004-2011 are generally solid with proper maintenance. The OM642 3.0L V6 diesel is extremely durable. The M276 V6 in newer models is also reliable. Known issues include balance shaft sprocket wear on M272/M273 and injector seal leaks on the diesel. AUAPW.ORG verifies all Mercedes engines.
