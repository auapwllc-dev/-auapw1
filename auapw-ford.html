Ford History

Ford — Complete Brand History

Ford Motor Company was founded by Henry Ford and incorporated on June 16, 1903, in Dearborn, Michigan. Henry Ford was 39 years old when he founded the company with 12 investors and $28,000 in cash. The company sold its first car, a two-cylinder Model A, on July 23, 1903 — and by October 1 had turned a profit of $37,000. Ford introduced the iconic Model T in 1908, designed to be affordable, durable, and easy to maintain. Demand was so great that Ford introduced the world's first moving assembly line at the Highland Park plant in 1913, revolutionizing industrial manufacturing globally. By 1923, Ford was producing more than half of all cars in the United States. Over 15 million Model Ts were produced before production ended in 1927. Ford introduced the first V8 engine in 1932. The Thunderbird debuted in 1954 as America's first personal luxury car. The Mustang launched in 1964 created the entire pony car segment — it remains a cultural icon today. The F-Series truck has been America's best-selling vehicle for over 40 consecutive years. Ford was the first automaker to pay workers $5 per day in 1914, doubling the prevailing wage. During WWII, Ford produced B-24 bombers at Willow Run at the rate of one per hour. Today Ford is investing $50 billion+ in electrification through 2026, launching the Mustang Mach-E, F-150 Lightning, and E-Transit.

Ford Parts FAQ

What are the most reliable Ford engines?
The 5.0L Coyote V8 (2011-present) and the 6.7L Power Stroke diesel are considered among Ford's most reliable engines. The 3.5L EcoBoost V6 is also well-regarded after early generation improvements. The classic 302/5.0L engine from older F-150s and Mustangs is legendary for longevity.
What Ford transmissions are most commonly replaced?
The 6R80 6-speed and 10R80 10-speed automatics are popular replacements for F-150 owners. The PowerShift dual-clutch DCT used in older Focus and Fiesta models had known issues and is frequently replaced. AUAPW.ORG carries all Ford transmission variants.
How much does a used Ford F-150 engine cost?
A used 5.0L Coyote V8 engine typically runs $1,500-$3,500 depending on mileage and year. The 3.5L EcoBoost runs $1,200-$2,800. We source from 2,000+ verified yards to get you the best price with a 90-180 day warranty.
Are Ford EcoBoost engines reliable used?
The 2.7L and 3.5L EcoBoost engines are generally reliable when properly maintained. Look for units with under 80,000 miles and full service history. AUAPW.ORG verifies mileage and condition before sourcing any engine for you.
